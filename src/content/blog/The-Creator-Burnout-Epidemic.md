"Consistency is key." It is the most common piece of advice given to new creators, and also the most dangerous. In the race to appease the algorithm, creators are working 80-hour weeks filming, editing, writing, and manually publishing across half a dozen platforms.

The result? Massive burnout. But the top 1% of creators aren't working harder than you; they are heavily leveraging automation.

## The Anatomy of Creator Burnout

Burnout doesn't come from the creative process; it comes from the administrative friction surrounding it. Downloading a video from TikTok, removing the watermark, writing a new caption, opening Instagram, uploading the Reel, opening YouTube, uploading the Short... doing this daily is soul-crushing.

## Step 1: Batch Creation
Never film just one video. Block out a four-hour window once a week to script, film, and rough-cut 5 to 10 pieces of short-form content. Keep your lighting set up, stay in the creative zone, and eliminate the daily pressure to perform.
